One parent brand, four services — written in Baybayin, the pre-colonial script of the Philippines. ᜆ is Ta, the first sound of Tabang. Each service takes its own syllable in the same tile; colour and character tell them apart. The script is the symbol — no illustration required.

Rooted in Baybayin
ᜆ

Baybayin is an indigenous Philippine writing system. Tabang’s mark is simply its name’s first syllable, set in a warm squircle. Vowels shift with a small mark called a kudlit — which is how Tabang (ᜆ, Ta) becomes Tugon (ᜆᜓ, Tu): the parent, grown into a service.
